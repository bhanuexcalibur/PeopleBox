01 / Positioning Audit

Where Peoplebox sits,
where it should.

Nova competes in a category that's being redrawn. The tools buyers compare it to were built for a different problem. That gap is the opportunity.

The category it gets lumped into
"AI video interviewing"

HireVue and Spark Hire own this frame. It positions Nova as a recording tool with smarter playback. Buyers default to price comparisons and "do we really need this" conversations. Wrong shelf entirely.

The category it actually competes in
"AI that runs your first-round hiring"

Nova doesn't record an interview: it conducts one. It follows up, scores, probes edge cases in coding rounds, and delivers a decision-ready report. That's closer to replacing a recruiter's first 40 hours than replacing a video camera.

The narrative gap
Built for volume, not just speed

Most competitors anchor on speed: "hire faster." That resonates with TA teams but barely moves VPs of Ops or CFOs. Nova's real value is cost per qualified hire at scale, a frame that unlocks budget conversations outside the HR silo.

The positioning thesis
Nova is the interview, not the tool around it

Competitors automate the logistics of interviewing. Nova automates the interview itself. This distinction matters enormously to high-volume buyers who've already tried scheduling tools and still have 300 unreviewed applications on Monday morning.

02 / GTM Brief

Two products,
two distinct motions.

Nova and the talent management platform serve overlapping companies but very different buyers. Conflating them in GTM dilutes both. Here's how I'd run them separately while keeping the story coherent.

01
Nova: Volume-hiring teams as the wedge

The buyers with the sharpest pain are high-turnover industries: logistics, BPO, retail ops, SaaS support. They're screening 200+ candidates per role, losing good people to ghosting, and haven't found a tool that actually interviews. Start here. Land, prove time-to-hire compression, expand upward.

02
Talent Platform: Expand into existing accounts

Once Nova lands in an org, the talent management platform becomes a natural second conversation. The data Nova generates (candidate quality, funnel health, time-to-shortlist) creates continuity into performance reviews and OKR tracking. The pitch: "same platform that hired them, now helps you grow them."

03
Middle East: Localization before expansion

The JD mentions US and ME. These are not the same motion. Middle East GTM needs compliance-first messaging (deepfake detection, structured proctoring, bias-free frameworks), local case studies, and partnership with regional HR tech communities before any broad push. Building brand in ME without credibility anchors first is expensive.

ICP definition: who gets the most value from Nova today

Not every company Nova could sell to is worth chasing. These three segments have the right combination of volume, urgency, and budget to close fast.

Segment A
High-volume, high-turnover ops teams

Logistics, BPO, retail, SaaS support. Hiring 50+ people per quarter. Recruiter bandwidth is the binding constraint, not budget.

Pain: "We're screening the same kind of candidate 400 times and we still miss good ones."

Segment B
Growth-stage SaaS scaling into new markets

50–500 employees. Hiring across functions simultaneously. TA team is 1–3 people trying to serve 12 open reqs.

Pain: "Every req is a firefight. We can't screen fast enough and panels are burned out."

Segment C
Tech hiring managers: first-round coding

Engineering teams replacing take-home assignments or manual technical screens. The live coding round with AI probing is the hook here, not the HR workflow.

Pain: "We give candidates a 3-hour assignment. Half ghost us. The other half are coached."

Launch hypothesis

Nova's live coding round launched in December 2025. That's a category-creating moment worth its own GTM push. The angle: not "AI coding assessment" but "the first interview where the AI actually challenges your code, not just tests it." A dedicated landing page, two to three customer stories from technical hiring managers, and a short LinkedIn content series from the founders would give this the surface area it deserves.

03 / Messaging Framework

Same product.
Three conversations.

The value Nova delivers looks different depending on who's buying it. These three message sets are designed to be independently coherent, not a diluted version of one master message.

Buyer A
VP Talent / TA Lead
Your interview panel is spending 80% of their time on candidates you'll reject. Nova fixes that before they even meet them.

Nova screens, interviews, and scores candidates end-to-end. By the time your team sees a name, it's already passed a structured first round with real follow-up questions, not a multiple-choice form. Time-to-first-shortlist drops from weeks to under 48 hours.

↳ Proof point: Most customers reduced time-to-first-interview report from 4–5 weeks to under 48 hours.
Buyer B
CFO / VP Operations
Every unscreened resume that sits in your queue is a cost. Nova's not a recruiting tool; it's a cost-per-qualified-hire equation.

At volume, the math is simple: manual screening costs recruiter hours at every stage. Nova compresses the top of the funnel without adding headcount. You get a higher-quality shortlist at a lower cost of production, especially as hiring volumes spike.

↳ Proof point: Nova reduces recruiter screening time by up to 90% (Peoplebox.ai product data).
Buyer C
Hiring Manager / Eng Lead
Nova's coding round doesn't just run the test. It challenges how candidates think: live, in conversation, with follow-up questions.

Traditional technical screens either take-home (easy to cheat, painful to review) or synchronous (panel bandwidth is finite). Nova runs a live conversational coding round: candidates write real code, Nova probes their logic and edge cases, and you get a structured report. No prep required from your side.

↳ Proof point: Launched Dec 2025, replacing three separate hiring stages with one continuous automated flow.

Objection: "Candidates will feel uncomfortable talking to an AI."

Flip it: candidates get an interview the same day they apply, at a time that works for them, with no scheduling friction. In early pilots, 87% said they felt more comfortable with Nova than with a recruiter. The candidates who complain about AI interviews are usually the ones who had a bad experience with a clunky video form, not a real conversation.

Objection: "We already use HireVue / Greenhouse / [ATS]."

Nova integrates with both. It's not a replacement for your ATS, it's the interview layer your ATS doesn't have. Your team keeps their existing stack; Nova adds the part where someone actually talks to the candidate.

Objection: "We're not sure the scoring is reliable."

Fair. Nova uses structured question frameworks and role-specific scoring rubrics, not black-box AI sentiment scoring. The report shows you the exact questions asked, the candidate's responses, and the rationale behind each score. Your team reviews it and makes the call: Nova just removes the 40 hours of work before that review.

04 / Sales Enablement

What the sales team
needs to win more deals.

Positioning and messaging only matter if the people selling the product can actually use them. These are the four pieces I'd build in the first 60 days.

🃏
Nova Competitive Battle Card
For reps in competitive deals
HireVue, Paradox, TestGorilla one-pagers with "they say / we say" structure for each objection pattern
One killer differentiator per competitor to anchor the rep's story, not a feature matrix
Specific call out for the live coding round vs. traditional take-home assessments, targeting eng hiring managers separately
Quote-ready stat bank: 48-hour pipeline compression, 90% recruiter time reduction, 87% candidate comfort metric
📄
Buyer-Specific One-Pagers
TA Lead / CFO / Hiring Manager versions
Three separate one-pagers, each speaking to the buyer's actual metric: time-to-hire, cost-per-hire, interview quality and signal reliability
Each one designed to be shared by the champion internally, not just left as a sales leave-behind
Deliberately short: one page, three proof points, one clear next step. Built for sharing in a Slack message, not a folder.
🔁
Win / Loss Interview Program
Quarterly insight engine
Structured discovery calls with 5 recent wins and 5 recent losses each quarter, looking for repeating patterns in the deal narrative
Specific focus: which objections are coming up most, which competitors are showing up late in deals, and which proof points are landing hardest
Output feeds directly into battle card updates and messaging refreshes, not a report that sits in a folder
🎯
Demo Narrative Framework
Turning live demos into AHA moments
The product demo should start with a real pain: "You have 300 applications. Show me what happens in the next 30 minutes." Then let Nova do it live.
Three demo tracks: TA lead track (pipeline view), CFO track (cost model, time compression), Eng hiring manager track (live coding round walkthrough)
Train reps to resist the feature-tour trap: demo the outcome, not the settings panel
